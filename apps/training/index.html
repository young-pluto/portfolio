Day 1: Chest, Shoulders (Side), Triceps, Abs

Flat Smith Machine Bench Press 4 sets × 8-12 reps

Execution Cues

  1. Setup:
    • Adjust a flat bench under the machine so that, when lying down, the handles/bar align with your mid to lower chest.
    • Ensure your feet are planted firmly and your back maintains a slight natural arch (not excessive).
    • Retract and depress your scapulae (shoulder blades down and back) to stabilize the upper body.
  2. Unrack & Starting Position:
    • Grip the handles firmly, with the bar directly above your mid to lower chest.
    • Elbows should be slightly tucked (~45 degrees from the torso).
  3. Eccentric (Lowering):
    • Lower the weight under control—slower than your push—to emphasize eccentric loading.
    • Descend until the handles are just above or lightly touch your lower chest.
    • You should feel a deep stretch in the chest at the bottom.
    • Pause for a split second at the bottom—do not bounce.
  4. Concentric (Pushing):
    • Push the weight explosively from the bottom position, focusing on driving through the palms.
    • Intentionally engage the chest—minimize shoulder and tricep dominance by maintaining scapular retraction and internal focus on pec contraction.
    • Do not lock out fully; stop just short to keep constant tension.
  5. Breathing:
    • Inhale on the way down, exhale forcefully as you push.
Incline Dumbbell Flyes 4 sets × 10-12 reps (superset last 2 sets with Push-Ups)

Execution Cues:

  • Set the bench between 15–30 degrees (no higher).
  • Lower the dumbbells slowly and under control — feel a deep stretch across your chest.
  • Keep the movement strict; do not turn the fly into a press.
  • Keep triceps relaxed; chest should do all the work.
  • Focus on bringing your elbows together at the top to maximize chest contraction.
Single-Arm Overhead Cable Triceps Extension 4 sets × 10-12 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Grab the round handle firmly and position your arm overhead.
  • Fully extend the elbow at the top, locking out under control.
  • Lower the handle slowly until your forearm nearly touches your bicep.
  • Keep the upper arm stable and pointed vertically throughout the movement.
  • Stretch deeply at the bottom, then drive the handle back up by isolating the triceps.
Single-Arm Cable Lateral Raises 3 sets × 12-15 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Grab the upright support pole with your non-working hand for balance.
  • Lean slightly toward the cable stack to increase the range of motion.
  • Start with your working hand positioned near your hip/pocket.
  • Raise the arm out and slightly above parallel, leading with the elbow.
  • Focus on lifting through the lateral delt; forearm remains passive (just a lever).
  • Lower the weight slowly and under control; lift explosively on the concentric phase.
  • Keep the torso stable throughout — avoid swinging or using momentum.
Seated Ab Crunch Machine 3 sets × 15-20 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Grip the handles above your head lightly, using only your fingers — avoid pulling with your arms.
  • Slightly lift your glutes off the seat to adjust your position, then firmly press your back against the seat.
  • Engage your legs to lock them in place — this ensures stability throughout the movement.
  • Begin the crunch by contracting your abs, keeping the movement focused solely on your core.
  • Avoid using your arms, forearms, or biceps — only your abs should be working.
  • Control the movement to maintain constant tension on your abs.
  • Do not fully extend or release — stop before the weight touches the stack to keep tension on the muscles.
  • Focus on a strong contraction at the top, then slowly lower to stretch the abs fully before initiating the next rep.
  • Keep your mind-muscle connection exclusively on the abs throughout the set.

Day 2: Back, Biceps, Rear Delts

Neutral-Grip Lat Pulldown 4 sets × 10-12 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Take a shoulder-width or slightly wider grip — not too close, not excessively wide.
  • Use a false grip (wrap fingers only, no thumb) to better connect to the lats.
  • Focus entirely on driving your elbows down toward your ribs and pockets.
  • Keep biceps, shoulders, and forearms relaxed; the lats should do all the work.
  • Pull down powerfully and contract your lats hard at the bottom.
  • Control the eccentric — release slowly to a full stretch without losing tension.
  • Maintain a mostly upright torso; a slight lean back is fine, but avoid turning it into a row.
  • Breathe properly (exhale on pull, inhale on release) and train with controlled aggression.
Straight-Arm Cable Lat Pushdown 3 sets × 15 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Position the bar across the middle of your palms for a firm grip.
  • Maintain a slight bend in your elbows throughout the movement.
  • Lean slightly forward to stabilize your torso and stay balanced under the cable tension.
  • Initiate the movement by driving your elbows down and back toward your lats.
  • Squeeze and contract your lats hard at the bottom of each rep.
  • Control the bar on the way up; don't let the weight pull you out of position.
  • It's normal to feel slight tricep activation, but the primary focus must remain on the lats.
  • Perform with high focus and controlled tempo, targeting 15–17 reps per set. YOUR LATS SHOULD BE ON FIRE. IF NOT YOU ARE DOING IT WRONG.
Incline Dumbbell Bicep Curls 4 sets × 10-12 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Set the bench at a 15–30 degree incline and lie back fully supported.
  • Use lighter weights (7.5–10 kg) to maintain strict form and tension.
  • Start with arms fully extended; let the biceps stretch completely at the bottom.
  • Keep shoulders pinned back and stable — no swinging or shoulder involvement.
  • Curl the dumbbells up by contracting the biceps only; elbows stay locked in place.
  • As you curl up, slightly supinate (rotate) your pinky upward to maximize bicep peak contraction.
  • Squeeze hard at the top, then lower slowly and under control to achieve a full stretch.
  • In the final few reps (only when close to failure), slight momentum from shoulders is acceptable — but keep it controlled.
Bent-Over Cable Rear Delt Flyes 3 sets × 10-15 reps

Execution Cues:

  • Set the cable handle at the lowest position on the pulley stack.
  • Stand a few steps away from the cable machine to increase range of motion.
  • Bend at the hips about 45–60 degrees, keeping your back flat and core braced.
  • Slightly rotate your torso toward the opposite side (away from the working arm) to better target the rear delts.
  • Grab the handle with a neutral grip (palm facing in).
  • Initiate the movement by pulling the handle out and back, leading with your elbow.
  • Focus purely on moving through the rear delts — avoid using your arms, forearms, or jerking the weight.
  • Stretch fully at the bottom and contract hard at the top.
  • Use very light weight — 1–2 plates for a standard pulley, or more if using a multiple-pulley system (like in Anytime Fitness setups).
  • Maintain strict form: slow eccentric, controlled concentric, no momentum.
